PublisherApress

Reviewer:Gabor Szabo
Review date:2003 Feruary
Rating:Very Good for independent programmers !
When I first saw the title of the book I was not sure what can be so special about writing Perl module for CPAN from just plain writing modules that warrants a whole book. The simple tast of regitering and PAUSE and then uploading your cipped module isn't such a difficult task.

Then reading the book gave me a couple of answeres. This books is not for novices but it is not for the experts either. It is mainly for those lone programmers who don't have a huge software engineering background. They will learn a great deal about SE in general and

Sam Tregar, the author is not new to Perl or to CPAN. I have been using his HTML::Template module for ages already and I like it very much so he must know a few things about the subject.

The book starts with the obvious question why to contribute to CPAN at all. Those who are reading this book probably have some reason already so for them its is just reassuring their motives.
Then it goes on with a light introduction to CPAN - its history, the technology behind it, its structure and how to install modules that were published on CPAN using the related CPAN.pm module or the ppm command.
Though it introduces the configuration of CPAN.pm it does not mention how to save the new configration information. Does not answer the question how to select but it is about writing anyway 17 explain when not to use the latest 23 suggest require in the wrong place suddenly jumps to explain BEGIN with no real reason does not explain the dangers in importing then goet to packages

The second chapter is a good overview of module creation. 35 suggests Carp 44-destroy only called at exit? Ch2 Some extreme programming in the back door
